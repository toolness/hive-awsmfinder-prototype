Opportunities abound for teens this winter in NYC! Whether looking to level up leadership skills, build out an art portfolio, connect with and learn from industry professionals in design and gaming industries, or just to meet new people, middle and high school students will find excellent after-school programs being offered across the city. Many of these opportunities are free (except where noted) and you can apply or register online! It’s sure to be a rewarding and valuable experience–click on these links or scroll down for more details!



  • Teen Center at the Vanderbilt YMCA–physical activity, community mapping and more
  • Playable Fashion at Eyebeam–where gaming, technology and fashion intersect
  • Geek Girls at The Lower Eastside Girls Club of NY–geek out with science, tech and engineering
  • Arts programs at DreamYard–theater, hip hop MCing, arts and activism
  • Intergenerational Media Literacy with The LAMP –critique and remix media with ageist stereotypes
  • Design the DOE High School Directory cover!
  • DesignPrep @ The Movies with Cooper-Hewitt–explore design inspiration in filmmaking
  • DesignPrep Poster Design with Rich Tu at Cooper-Hewitt
  • DesignPrep Unconventional Fashion with Sena Yang at Cooper-Hewitt–learn draping and pattern-making with unique materials
  • Spring Documentary Filmmaking Lab at Reel Works Teen Filmmaking
  • Teen Tech Time at Brooklyn Public Library–gaming, blogging, LEGO Mindstorms and more
  • DIY Makerspace at Brooklyn Public Library–3D printing and physical computing
  • Adaptive Gaming Arcade at Brooklyn Public Library
  • Creative Activism in Climate Change at the Zoo–explore urban ecology and sustainability
  • The Academy at Urban Arts Partnership–spoken word, coding, hip hop dance, photography and a lot more

 

Teen Center at Vanderbilt YMCA

  • Located at 224 E. 47th Street, NY, NY 10017
  • Mondays-Fridays 3pm-6pm (Until May 16th)

Teen Center engages youth in meaningful activities that allow them to develop socially, physically, and academically. Fitness programs, community mapping, leadership skills and more! Teen Center programming is free and open to teens ages 12-19 currently attending middle school or high school. Click for more information or view the flyer.

 

Playable Fashion at Eyebeam

  • Located at 540 W 21st St, New York, NY 10011
  • After-school program runs February – June 2014

Playable Fashion is a free program for teens ages 13-19 to explore the intersection of fashion, technology, and game design, and to learn the concepts and techniques behind them in order to create their own digital and/or physical games and wearable technology projects. Game designers and Eyebeam Teaching Fellows Kaho Abe and Ramsey Nasser are teaching this program with Global Kids’ Juan Rubio, who will guide students in creating their games’ story around personal, social, or global issues. Teens will showcase their work at Eyebeam’s Game Arcade in May and at Emoti-Con! in June 2014. Sign up here or find out more!

 

Geek Girls at The Lower Eastside Girls Club of NY

  • After-school with some weekend workshops–dates and times vary.

Geek Girls is a STEM-based experiential education program for LESGC members ages 11-14. Apply today to become a LESGC member!

Intergenerational Media Literacy (IML) with The LAMP, OATS and WNET

  • Located at Senior Planet Center, 127 W. 25th St., New York, NY 10011
  • Tuesdays and Thursdays, from 4pm-6pm

Senior citizens and teenagers learn how to spot ageist messages in media, and collaborate to make remixes calling out harmful, ageist stereotypes. Together they will create a multimedia campaign to tell the world that this treatment is not okay, and urge media producers to change their ways. Register online.Learn more.

 

NYC DOE Cover Design Competition

  • Accepting designs from Monday, January 27, 2014 through Friday, February 14, 2014

Every year, the New York City Department of Education distributes thousands of school directories to students and their families throughout New York City. The Cover Design Competition encourages high school students to reflect on their lives as public school students in New York City and express their ideas artistically in the form of a cover design. This competition is an opportunity for high school students to work with a real-world client, learn how to develop a design with a professional designer, and gain practical creative experience they can take with them after high school. For 9th-12th grade students. Smithsonian Cooper-Hewitt National Design Museum is accepting submissions until February 14th.

 

DesignPrep @ The Movies with Smithsonian, Cooper-Hewitt National Design Museum

  • Located at Cooper-Hewitt Design Center, 111 Central Park North, New York, NY 10026
  • First Friday of every month from 4:00pm-7:30pm

Design can affect the look and feel of a film. It can inspire films about particular design disciplines, like fashion or architecture. Other times a director’s vision inspires, or even parallels, design and innovation being used in our everyday lives. Join DesignPrep for a night at the movies. Meet up with friends and make new ones while enjoying a free movie and PIZZA. For 9th-12th grade students. Register free online.

DesignPrep: Unconventional Fashion with Sena Yang at Smithsonian, Cooper-Hewitt National Design Museum

  • Located at Cooper-Hewitt Design Center, 111 Central Park North, New York, NY

2014 Spring Documentary Filmmaking Lab at Reel Works Teen Filmmaking

  • Located at 540 President Street, Suite 2F, Brooklyn, NY 11215 in Gowanus, Brooklyn
  • Wednesdays and Fridays from 4:30pm-6:30pm
  • February 5th through mid-August

The Lab is an intensive, six-month, afterschool workshop offered FREE to New York City youth (ages 14 – 20). Each semester, they select 14 aspiring filmmakers who know they have an amazing story inside of them they can’t wait to tell the world! Every student makes a short documentary film working one-on-one with a professional filmmaker. It’s a crash course in documentary filmmaking using the latest digital video technology – including HDV cameras and Final Cut Pro editing software. Each student will write, produce, direct and edit a short film that is personal, powerful and real. Apply online.

Creative Activism in Climate Change with Wildlife Conservation Society

  • Located at the Bronx Zoo, Prospect Park Zoo, Central Park Zoo and Queens Zoo
  • Program runs between February and May, twice per week after school from 4-6:30pm, plus four Saturday sessions from 9:30am-4:30pm
  • Teens receive 60 hours of instruction; free transportation; snacks at after-school sessions; and, a $125 stipend

This is a free program for teens, ages 14-18, to conduct research in urban ecology and climate change to better understand sustainability issues in our city and to produce a relevant, scientifically accurate project that will connect people to conservation issues facing NYC. Digital Media Artists, Dr. Eric Sanderson, Jonah Brucker-Cohen, Michael Heimbinder and Paolo Cirio along with WCS staff will guide students in exploring different methods for expression that motivate people in the digital realm to make meaningful contributions to important causes. Find more information or register online.
